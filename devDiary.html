MSC2018: Advanced Media Design Technologies Course Website

Week 1: February 3-7

As the project lead for our group project, I spent most of the past week organizing the draft design document and consolidating my assigned section of research to bring to our group meeting on Thursday. Early on in our project planning, I had volunteered to organize the mood board, stylesheet and UI design elements for the design document before I had volunteered as team lead. I figured I wouldn’t be as busy doing a few admin tasks so taking on the UI design and stylesheet wouldn’t be too much on my plate. This week, everyone was tasked in bringing their slice of content research that they were assigned as well as pitching their general idea of how we may go about creating the AR project. The meeting went as planned and almost everyone was present with their research and ideas, all of which were very comprehensive and in-depth! I had pitched to create a simple sloped 3D printed hexagonal environment with “divots” to place “effector tokens” on that would have scannable images pasted on. It was suggested that the main piece may have to be flat to best accommodate the AR projection, so we went with a simpler flat hexagonal piece. A member of our group, Max, was unable to contribute to the meeting, so I reassigned them to help complete a different section of the project as to accommodate for their absence. I am working on a more efficient method to check in with the rest of the group. So far, we’ve been using our discord chat to keep in contact and put up drafts for feedback and this has been going well so far. I have implemented preliminary deadlines for drafts to ensure that everyone has feedback they can work off and so far, most of our group members have been able to reach these deadlines. After meeting on Thursday, we agreed to meet with Alex on Friday to go over a few of the ideas we had pitched as a group. I now feel as though we have a strong foundation in which we may begin developing the application. My challenge now is to figure out how best to assist the rest of the team as I am not directly involved in much of the development/production. I have assigned myself the responsibility of putting together clips from the development team for the development walkthrough as well as putting together the design document, final presentation, and maintaining contact with Alex as my primary duties. I’ll also try to contribute sketches and feedback notes throughout the production process to further involve myself in the production process. I am disappointed to an extent; I had joined this group to lend myself as a 3D production artist and a I have ended up doing administrative tasks instead as a group lead. I do feel equipped to be a project lead, so I believe this is the best way I can contribute to our project, especially since there are quite a few group members. Week 2: February 10-14

For week 2 as project lead, I began the week by reviewing the meeting minutes from the team meeting I had been unable to attend and checked with my group members on the previsualization components they had been assigned. I continued to refine and contribute to the introductory and design sections and added any finished drafts to the main document. A few of the drafts I had updated the design document with needed to be revised so I held off updating these sections. I reminded my group members to contribute any draft sections they were responsible for and to update any revised documents on the main document by the end of the day on February 13th as to allow myself some time to look over the final document and proof-read the draft for submission. The day before the submission, I held a brief update meeting with a few of my group members to update each other on the status of each of the drafts and the timeline leading up to the final submission the day after. The morning of the submission I confirmed with my group members that I would be submitting the final document at noon and consolidated any edits to the draft document. Additionally, I planned for a quick asynchronous check-in with my group members sometime mid-way through the reading week just to reach out to see if there were any touch points, we could discuss or if another meeting would be necessary to consolidate any knowledge before beginning production.

Week 3: February 17-21

For week 3 as project lead, I began formatting the After Effects files for the developers walkthrough demo video and created a document for the final presentation. I hadn’t been able to work on much throughout the reading week but I was able to check-in with my group members mid week to touch-base on how everything was going with the project. We planned to meet more formally on Tuesday during class to check-in on production after the reading week and make sure that everyone has what they need to continue to work on the project. Around the end of the reading week, Jeah was able to show us a sneak-peak of the app so far and it appears to be functioning quite well so far! I am very excited for how the rest of the project will develop and am looking forward to further contributing to the documentation and project scheduling. Additionally, I spent some of the reading week reading up on Unity and familiarizing myself with a few of the aspects to consider during the development process. I also referred o the detailed developer notes that Jeah provided to better equip myself with the knowledge to help with the dev process as needed.
